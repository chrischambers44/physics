Energy Resources

Renewable and non-renewable energy sources

Learn

Learning Objectives: Describe different energy resources, distinguish between renewable and non-renewable sources, and understand their environmental impacts.

Main Energy Resources on Earth

The main energy resources available include:

  • Fossil fuels (coal, oil, and gas)
  • Nuclear fuel
  • Bio-fuel
  • Wind
  • Hydro-electricity
  • Geothermal
  • Tides
  • The Sun (solar)
  • Water waves

Renewable vs Non-Renewable

Renewable Energy Resource: One that is being (or can be) replenished as it is used. It won't run out.
✓ Renewable Resources:
  • Wind
  • Solar (Sun)
  • Hydro-electricity
  • Tides
  • Waves
  • Geothermal
  • Bio-fuels
✗ Non-Renewable Resources:
  • Coal
  • Oil
  • Natural gas
  • Nuclear fuel (uranium, plutonium)

These will eventually run out. They are being used up faster than they are being formed.

Uses of Energy Resources

Transport:
  • Petrol/diesel (from oil) - cars, lorries, planes
  • Electricity - electric cars, trains
  • Bio-fuels - some cars and buses
Electricity Generation:
  • Fossil fuels - coal, gas power stations
  • Nuclear - nuclear power stations
  • Renewable - wind turbines, solar panels, hydro-electric dams
Heating:
  • Gas - central heating in homes
  • Electricity - electric heaters
  • Wood/bio-fuel - stoves and fires
  • Solar - solar thermal panels for hot water

Reliability

Reliable sources: Available all the time when needed
  • Fossil fuels - can be burned whenever needed
  • Nuclear - runs continuously
  • Bio-fuels - can be stored and burned when needed
Less reliable sources: Depend on conditions
  • Wind - only when it's windy
  • Solar - only during daytime and when sunny
  • Waves - variable with weather
  • Tides - predictable but only at certain times

Environmental Impact

Fossil Fuels:
  • Release CO₂ (contributes to climate change)
  • Release sulfur dioxide (causes acid rain)
  • Particulates cause air pollution
  • Oil spills damage ecosystems
Nuclear:
  • No CO₂ emissions during operation
  • Produces radioactive waste (difficult to dispose safely)
  • Risk of accidents (though rare)
Renewable Sources:
  • Wind turbines - noise, visual impact, harm to birds
  • Hydro-electric - flooding of valleys, affects habitats
  • Solar panels - lots of space needed, visual impact
  • Generally much lower environmental impact than fossil fuels

Trends in Energy Use

Patterns and trends include:

  • Global energy demand is increasing
  • Use of renewable energy is growing
  • Coal use is declining in many countries
  • Natural gas use increasing as "bridge" fuel
  • More electric vehicles reducing oil use for transport
Science vs Policy: Science can identify environmental issues from energy use, but cannot always solve them alone. Political, social, ethical, and economic factors also affect decisions about which energy sources to use.
