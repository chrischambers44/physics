Energy Stores and Systems

Understanding how energy is stored and transferred

Learn

Learning Objective: Describe all the changes involved in the way energy is stored when a system changes, for common situations.

What is a System?

A system is an object or group of objects that we are studying. When we look at energy changes, we need to define what our system is.

Examples of systems:
  • A ball being thrown
  • A kettle boiling water
  • A car and its surroundings
  • The Earth and Moon

Energy Stores

Energy can be stored in different ways within a system:

Energy Store Description Example
Kinetic Energy in moving objects A moving car, flowing water
Gravitational Potential Energy stored in objects raised above the ground A book on a shelf, water at the top of a waterfall
Elastic Potential Energy stored in stretched or compressed objects Stretched spring, compressed rubber band
Thermal Energy stored in hot objects Hot water, warm radiator
Chemical Energy stored in chemical bonds Food, batteries, fuel
Nuclear Energy stored in atomic nuclei Uranium fuel, Sun's core
Electrostatic Energy stored in charged objects Rubbed balloon, thunderclouds
Magnetic Energy stored in magnetic objects Two magnets near each other

Common Energy Changes

Key Point: Energy is never created or destroyed - it just moves between different stores. This is called the conservation of energy.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Object Projected Upwards

When you throw a ball upwards:

  • Start: Ball has kinetic energy (it's moving)
  • Rising: Kinetic energy decreases, gravitational potential energy increases
  • Top: All kinetic energy has transferred to gravitational potential energy (ball stops momentarily)
  • Falling: Gravitational potential energy transfers back to kinetic energy
  • Note: Some energy is transferred to thermal energy due to air resistance
Example 2: Moving Object Hitting an Obstacle

When a car crashes into a wall:

  • Before: Car has kinetic energy
  • During crash: Kinetic energy transfers to thermal energy (heat), sound energy, and elastic potential energy (car deforms)
  • After: Kinetic energy is zero - the car has stopped
Example 3: Object Accelerated by a Constant Force

When a car accelerates:

  • Chemical energy in fuel transfers to kinetic energy of the car
  • Some energy also transfers to thermal energy (engine heat, friction) and sound energy
Example 4: Vehicle Slowing Down

When a car brakes:

  • Kinetic energy transfers to thermal energy in the brakes
  • Some energy transfers to sound energy
  • The brakes get hot!
Example 5: Bringing Water to Boil in an Electric Kettle

When you boil a kettle:

  • Electrical energy from the mains transfers to thermal energy in the heating element
  • Thermal energy transfers to the water, increasing its temperature
  • Some thermal energy is wasted to the surroundings (kettle gets hot on the outside)
Remember: In all these examples, energy is transferred between different stores, but the total amount of energy stays the same. Some energy is often "wasted" as thermal energy that spreads to the surroundings.
