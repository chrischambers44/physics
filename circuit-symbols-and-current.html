Circuits and Current

Understanding electric circuits and charge flow

What is an Electric Circuit?

An electric circuit is a closed loop that allows electric charge to flow. For current to flow, the circuit must be complete and include a source of potential difference (like a battery).

Key Requirement: For electrical charge to flow through a closed circuit, the circuit must include a source of potential difference (voltage).

What is Electric Current?

Electric current is the flow of electrical charge. When a circuit is complete, electrons flow around it, carrying charge and energy.

Definition: Electric current is a flow of electrical charge. The size of the electric current is the rate of flow of electrical charge.

Current is measured in amperes (A), often shortened to "amps". An ammeter is used to measure current and must be connected in series (in line with) the component.

Charge Flow

The amount of charge that flows depends on the current and how long it flows for.

Q = I t
Q = charge flow (coulombs, C)
I = current (amperes, A)
t = time (seconds, s)
Remember: 1 coulomb is the amount of charge that flows when a current of 1 ampere flows for 1 second.

Worked Examples

Example 1: A current of 2 A flows through a circuit for 30 seconds. How much charge flows?

Solution:
Q = I t
Q = 2 × 30
Q = 60 C

Example 2: 450 C of charge flows through a lamp in 5 minutes. What is the current?

Solution:
First convert 5 minutes to seconds: 5 × 60 = 300 s

Q = I t, so I = Q/t
I = 450 ÷ 300
I = 1.5 A

Example 3: How long does it take for 120 C of charge to flow if the current is 0.5 A?

Solution:
Q = I t, so t = Q/I
t = 120 ÷ 0.5
t = 240 s (or 4 minutes)

Current in a Series Circuit

Important Rule: A current has the same value at any point in a single closed loop (series circuit).

This means if you measure the current at different points in a series circuit, you'll get the same reading. The current doesn't "get used up" as it goes around the circuit.

Why?

Think of it like water flowing through a pipe. The amount of water passing any point per second is the same all the way around. Charge works the same way - the same amount passes every point each second.
